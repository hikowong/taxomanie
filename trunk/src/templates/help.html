What can I do with PhyloExplorer ?

    You can check PHYLOGENETIC TREES (on the Homepage): 
    - by uploading a Newick or simple Nexus format file
- by entering a Newick or simple Nexus format tree in the textfield
    You can get STATISTICS about your collection of trees as soon as your collection is sent: 
    - General informations: shows the number of trees, the number of taxa (including bad taxa and homonyms).
- Correct your collection: enables you to set the right name of bad taxa and homonyms. Then click "Correct collection".
- Chart area: 2 charts are displayed
              --> The 1st one displays how full trees are.
              --> The 2nd one displays the distribution frequency of a taxon.
- NCBI taxonomy: every node of the whole collection are displayed with the NCBI arborescence.
              --> Every node has 2 numbers --> (number of its representatives, number of trees in which it is).
              --> Dark blue names are the species that are in YOUR collection.
              --> Light blue names are the commom parents of species.
              --> You can filter your collection thanks to the checkbox next to each name.
                  (If you check a box, it means you don't want this node in your collection)
                  Then click "Restrict collection" to filter.
    You can see a picture of a taxon when you put the mouse over its name 
    - Pictures are taken from WikiSpecies and/or WikiPedia
    You can QUERY the collection 
    - To search a taxon or node in the collection
- Example of query: {catarrhini}>3 and {murinae} and {bos} and not {glires}
  i.e: get every tree where "murinae" and "bos" are, but not "glires" and where the number of "catarrhini" is up to 3
- Syntax of query (upper or lower case is accepted):
                    --> {...}        {name you want to search} must be between braces. e.g: {mus musculus}
                    --> OR           {name 1} or {name 2} or {name 3} ...
                    --> AND          {name 1} and {name 2} and {name 3} ...
                    --> NOT          {name 1} and not {name 2} and not {name 3} ...
                    --> (...)        not {name 1} and not ({name 2} or {name 3}) ... use brackets for priority operations
                    --> <, >, <=, >= {name 1} > 2 and {name 2} < 4 ... e.g: {eutheria} > 4 and {murinae} < 3
                    --> ==           {name 1} == 3 ... e.g: {laurasiatheria} == 2
  Spaces between operands are accepted: {glires}>3 is equivalent to {glires} > 3. It's the same for ==, <=, >= ...
    You can obviously BROWSE YOUR COLLECTION 
    - Just click the "Browse collection" button
- Display tree: choose the tree you want to display thanks to the select menu
- Show parents: show intermediate parents of an intern node (usually hidden for visual comfort)
- Show/hide all parents: expand/hide all intermediate parents
    You can hide/show intern nodes in trees 
    - Just click the "Show/hide intern nodes" button above the Tree Name
- The tree will be displayed with/without the intern nodes (just show the structure of the user's original tree)
    You can DOWNLOAD your filtered collection 
    - Just click the "Download collection" button
    You can DOWNLOAD the NCBI taxonomy 
    - Just click the "Download NCBI tree" button
